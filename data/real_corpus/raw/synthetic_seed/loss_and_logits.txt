Loss and logits notes

A language model produces logits for every vocabulary item.
Logits are unnormalized scores, not probabilities.
Cross entropy compares those scores with the target token ids.

Small example:
- context: "deep learning uses"
- target token: "gradients"
- higher target logit usually means lower loss

Important reminders:
- finite loss matters more than low loss in the first bounded run
- debugging starts with shapes, dtype, and masking
- validation loss should be logged separately from training loss
